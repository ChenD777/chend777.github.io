Research

My research centers on energy-efficient and safe operation of data center cooling systems. I develop physics-informed learning models and advanced control methods that work under realistic system dynamics, uncertainties, and operational constraints.

Project 1: Physics-Informed Machine Learning Framework for Modeling (Aug 2022 – Jun 2024)

  • Proposed APCNNs with improved prediction accuracy and reduced computational cost compared to traditional PCNNs.
  • Reduced short-term (15-min) forecast MAE by ~20% and multi-day errors by ~80% on real-world data center datasets.
  • Established rigorous proofs to verify physical consistency across modeled processes.

Project 2: Physics-Informed ML-Based Model Predictive Control (Jun 2024 – Oct 2025)

  • Developed an APCNN-assisted MPC controller with ~20% cooling-energy reduction under normal and unseen high-load conditions.
  • Demonstrated consistently better energy efficiency with more accurate and stable temperature regulation than ML/time-series baselines.
  • Extended to physics-informed adaptive MPC (PIML-AMPC), achieving ~24% annual cooling-energy savings vs fixed-setpoint control.
  • Improved deployment readiness by integrating learning-based prediction with physics-based knowledge for robust operation.

Project 3: Deep Reinforcement Learning Control for Data Centers (Oct 2025 – Apr 2026)

  • Built an end-to-end DRL control platform for hybrid-cooled data centers with closed-loop training and evaluation.
  • Implemented and benchmarked SAC, TD3, and PPO, achieving ~15% energy savings while maintaining stable thermal performance.
  • Advanced safe DRL via constraint handling and violation monitoring to improve reliability under diverse conditions.
